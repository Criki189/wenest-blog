You step into the shower, turn the water on, and by the time you've finished rinsing your hair, you're standing in a shallow pool. The water drains eventually, but not fast enough. You're not imagining it.

By the end of this, you'll know the five things that actually fix a slow shower drain—and which one is probably your problem.

## The three things slowing your drain down

Most slow shower drains are caused by hair, soap scum, or a combination of both. Hair catches on the drain cover or the first bend in the pipe. Soap scum—especially from bar soap—coats the hair and hardens into a sticky mass. Over time, this builds up until water can barely get through.

In Sydney, there's a fourth factor: hard water. Sydney Water's hardness varies by suburb, but areas like the Inner West and parts of the North Shore see levels around 50–60 mg/L. That's not extreme, but it's enough to leave mineral deposits that make soap scum cling harder and narrow the pipe diameter over time.

The deeper blockage—the one that needs a plumber—is rare. If your shower drains slowly but clears within a few minutes, it's almost always surface-level. If water backs up into other fixtures or the drain gurgles, that's a waste pipe problem.

## Fix 1: Pull the drain cover and clear the hair trap

Start here. Most shower drains have a removable cover—either it unscrews, or it lifts straight out. Wear gloves. Pull out whatever's caught in the trap.

You'll usually find a clump of hair wound around soap residue. It's unpleasant, but it takes 30 seconds. Rinse the cover, replace it, run the shower for a minute. If the water drains normally, you're done.

If it's still slow, the blockage is deeper.

## Fix 2: Use a drain snake (not a chemical cleaner)

A basic plastic drain snake costs about $8 from Bunnings. Feed it into the drain, twist it as you push, then pull it back out slowly. You'll drag up hair and scum from the first 30–40cm of pipe.

We've stopped recommending chemical drain cleaners for showers. They rarely dissolve hair completely, they can damage older PVC pipes if used repeatedly, and they create caustic water sitting in your drain if the blockage doesn't shift. A plunger or snake is safer and more effective.

## Fix 3: Plunge the drain properly

If the snake doesn't work, try a plunger. Not the toilet plunger—use a flat-bottomed sink plunger, or a small cup plunger.

Block the overflow vent with a wet cloth (most showers don't have one, but check). Fill the shower base with 5–10cm of water. Place the plunger over the drain, press down firmly, then pull up sharply. Repeat 10–15 times.

The suction can dislodge blockages the snake can't reach. If water suddenly drains fast, you've cleared it.

## Fix 4: Flush with boiling water and vinegar

This won't clear a heavy blockage, but it works for soap scum buildup and biofilm—the layer of bacteria and residue that coats the inside of the pipe.

Boil a kettle. Pour 100ml of white vinegar down the drain. Wait 30 minutes. Then pour the boiling water down slowly, in two or three stages.

The vinegar breaks down soap scum and neutralises odours. The boiling water flushes it away. Do this once a month to keep the drain running cleanly. If your shower still drains slowly after this, the blockage is mechanical, not chemical.

## Fix 5: Check the drain trap and waste pipe

If none of the above works, the blockage is in the P-trap (the curved section of pipe under the shower base) or further down the waste line. This requires access to the plumbing, which usually means lifting floorboards or cutting into a wall cavity.

At this point, call a licensed plumber. Attempting to dismantle the trap yourself without knowing what you're doing can crack the pipe or break the seal, which creates a much larger problem.

## Frequently asked questions

### How do I know if my slow shower drain is serious?

If water pools above your ankles during a normal shower, or if the drain gurgles and backs up into other fixtures, it's beyond a surface blockage. That usually means the problem is in the waste pipe or further down the line—call a licensed plumber. Surface-level slowness that clears within a few minutes is almost always hair and soap scum.

### Can I use Drano or other chemical drain cleaners in my shower?

You can, but we've stopped recommending it. Chemical cleaners rarely dissolve hair completely, they can damage older PVC pipes if used repeatedly, and they create caustic water sitting in your drain if the blockage doesn't shift. A plunger or drain snake is safer and more effective for shower drains.

### Why does my shower drain smell even after I've cleared the blockage?

The smell is usually biofilm—a layer of bacteria, soap residue, and organic matter coating the inside of the pipe. Clearing the visible blockage doesn't remove the film. Pour 100ml of white vinegar down the drain, let it sit for 30 minutes, then flush with boiling water. Repeat weekly to keep the smell down.

### Does Sydney's hard water make shower drains worse?

Yes, especially in the Inner West and parts of the North Shore where Sydney Water's hardness levels sit around 50–60 mg/L. Hard water leaves mineral deposits that narrow the pipe over time and make soap scum stickier. If you're dealing with slow drains every few months, a water softener might be worth it—but that's a bigger conversation.
